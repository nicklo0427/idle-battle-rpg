# 暗域放置 — Dark Idle Reverie
### A Design Philosophy for 放置英雄

---

## I. The Movement

*暗域放置* — "Dark Idle Reverie" — is an aesthetic philosophy born from the collision of two silences: the silence of waiting, and the silence of dusk. It is the visual language of a game played in stolen moments — before dawn, during lunch, after midnight — and its imagery must carry that same suspended, liminal quality. Every composition is a held breath. Every color is the last light before darkness, or the first light after it.

The work is never decorative. It earns every pixel through painstaking constraint: no more than eight colors per scene, no gradient softer than a hard step could achieve, no edge unintentional. This is meticulous artisanal work — the kind that looks effortless only because the craft is so deep it has become invisible. Forms are read from ten meters. Mood is understood in one second.

---

## II. Space and Form

Space is divided with the authority of a woodblock print. Forms are silhouettes first — massive, confident shapes carved from shadow — with detail added only where the eye must rest. The horizon is always implied: a band of sky, a band of earth, a band of mystery between. Nothing floats without gravity. Everything has weight.

Architecture, nature, and character exist in the same visual grammar: all reduced to their essential geometry, all speaking the same tectonic language. A mountain and a warrior and a dungeon gate are all, at their core, a triangle against a rectangle. The philosophy is: simplify until the soul of the subject remains, then stop.

---

## III. Color and Material

Each region, each character, each mood has exactly one dominant color — its *soul hue* — from which all other colors are derived by darkening, desaturating, or adding a single warm or cool accent. Orange for the frontier. Steel-blue for the mine. Violet for the ancient. Indigo for the deep. Gold for the hero.

Color is not applied; it is *withheld*. The darkest value occupies the most space. The brightest accent — one or two pixels of pure gold, a sliver of cyan bioluminescence — earns its presence through scarcity. The compositions are dark by default, bright by exception. This restraint is not poverty; it is the mark of someone who has labored over countless calibrations to find the single color that completes the picture.

---

## IV. Rhythm and Repetition

Pixel art's great gift is repetition with variation. Tiles, bricks, stones, leaves, water ripples — all emerge from a single module repeated across a surface, each instance carrying tiny deliberate differences. This systematic accumulation is what gives the work its texture and its sense of time: you feel that someone sat with this surface for hours, placing each mark with care, because they did.

Atmospheric particles — dust, sparks, bubbles, embers — are scattered according to invisible grids, then offset by hand. The result reads as organic but feels ordered. It is an art of controlled entropy, and achieving it requires the patience of a master craftsman who understands that beauty lives in the thousand small decisions no single viewer will consciously notice.

---

## V. Typography as Sigil

Text in this world is a rare and serious thing. When it appears, it is treated as a sigil — a mark with power — not as information. It is set in monospaced or pixel-display fonts, always small, always in the warmest or coldest tone the palette allows, always at the edge of the composition where it can breathe. Never centered unless it is a name or a title of great importance. Never more than ten words. The typeface is chosen because it looks like it was stamped into stone or burned into wood, not because it was typed on a screen.
